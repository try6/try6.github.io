Research Interests

Scaling patterns, optimization, and evolution

Scaling Growth Theory

This line of research focuses on scaling as an empirical regularity in the growth of complex systems. Across domains, growth rates, fluctuations, and size distributions often exhibit robust scaling relations. By constructing parsimonious models, I aim to explain these patterns and identify the minimal generative mechanisms—such as proportional growth, heterogeneity, and constraints—that jointly give rise to observable regularities. The central question is why scaling emerges, and under what conditions it breaks down.

Self Similarity, and bridging micro rules and macro patterns

Multi-Scale Modeling

This line of research is methodological, aiming to develop modeling frameworks for capturing cross-scale dynamics in complex systems. The goal is to formalize how structure, interactions, and stochasticity propagate and couple across scales. I am particularly interested in building models that are both interpretable and tractable, linking micro-level rules, network structures, and high-dimensional dynamics to macro-level emergent behavior, and identifying low-dimensional structure in complex systems.
